What is known as a Bullet Train?

A bullet train is a high-speed passenger train designed to travel at extremely fast speeds on specially built tracks. Bullet trains usually operate at speeds between 250 km/h to 350 km/h, making them one of the fastest modes of land transport in the world.

Unlike regular trains, they run on dedicated rail lines, which means there are no road crossings or slow freight trains to interrupt their journey. Advanced signaling systems, modern braking technology, and strict safety standards make them one of the safest ways to travel.
